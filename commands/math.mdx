`/ads math` is a conversational PPC calculator. Ask Claude to run a specific calculation, provide your numbers, and it returns results with the formula shown, a benchmark comparison, and a plain-language recommendation. It works with pasted data from platform exports or numbers you type directly. No API access or spreadsheet required.

## Command

```bash
/ads math
```

There is no structured form to fill out. Describe what you want to calculate in plain language and Claude handles the rest.

**Example prompts:**

```
/ads math

What ROAS do I need to break even with 40% gross margins?
How much should I spend to get 50 leads at a $100 CPA?
We spent $12,000 and generated $41,000 in revenue — what's our ROAS?
Our LTV is $1,200 and we acquired 80 customers for $18,000 total spend — is that healthy?
```

<Note>
  `/ads math` is a quick conversational utility. Claude does not generate a full written report for math calculations and does not append the community footer after this command.
</Note>

## Calculators

### CPA — Cost Per Acquisition

```
CPA = Total Spend / Total Conversions
```

**Inputs needed:**
- Total ad spend for the period
- Total conversions for the same period

**Output:** CPA with period context, trend analysis if historical data is provided, comparison to industry benchmarks.

---

### ROAS — Return on Ad Spend

```
ROAS = Revenue from Ads / Ad Spend
ROAS% = (Revenue − Spend) / Spend × 100
```

**Inputs needed:**
- Total ad spend
- Total revenue attributed to ads

**Output:** ROAS as a ratio (e.g., 3.5×) and as a percentage (250%), break-even ROAS based on your margins, comparison to platform benchmarks.

---

### Break-even analysis

```
Break-Even CPA  = Average Order Value × Profit Margin
Break-Even ROAS = 1 / Profit Margin
```

**Inputs needed:**
- Average order value (AOV) or average deal value
- Gross margin percentage
- Current CPA or ROAS

**Output:** Maximum profitable CPA, minimum profitable ROAS, how much headroom exists between current performance and break-even, and whether to scale, maintain, or cut.

**Example:** With a 40% gross margin, your break-even ROAS is `1 / 0.40 = 2.5×`. Any campaign delivering below 2.5× ROAS is losing money on every conversion.

---

### Impression share opportunity

```
Revenue Opportunity = Current Revenue × (1 / Current IS − 1)
```

**Inputs needed:**
- Current impression share percentage
- Impression share lost to budget (%)
- Impression share lost to rank (%)
- Current spend and conversions

**Output:** Estimated additional conversions from capturing full impression share, budget required for full IS (estimated), and whether the priority is a budget increase or quality score improvement.

---

### Budget forecasting

```
Projected Spend       = Daily Budget × Days in Period
Projected Conversions = Projected Spend / Historical CPA
Projected Revenue     = Projected Conversions × AOV
```

Claude generates three scaling scenarios:

| Scenario | Budget change | Notes |
|----------|--------------|-------|
| Conservative | +20% | Safe scaling within the 20% rule |
| Moderate | +50% | Growth with diminishing returns warning |
| Aggressive | +100% | High growth, significant efficiency risk |

**Inputs needed:** Current daily budget, historical CPA (last 30 days), forecast period, and AOV if revenue projection is needed.

---

### LTV:CAC ratio

```
CAC           = Total Marketing Spend / New Customers Acquired
LTV           = Average Revenue per Customer × Average Customer Lifespan
LTV:CAC Ratio = LTV / CAC
```

**Inputs needed:**
- Total marketing spend across all channels
- New customers acquired in the same period
- Average revenue per customer (monthly or annual)
- Average customer lifespan in months
- Gross margin (optional, for unit economics)

**Interpreting the ratio:**

| LTV:CAC | Interpretation |
|---------|---------------|
| < 1:1 | Losing money on every customer acquired |
| 1:1 – 2:1 | Break-even to marginal; unsustainable long-term |
| 3:1 | Healthy — the SaaS industry benchmark |
| 5:1+ | May be under-investing in growth; consider increasing spend |

Claude also calculates your payback period: the number of months to recover the cost of acquiring a customer.

---

### MER — Marketing Efficiency Ratio

```
MER = Total Revenue / Total Marketing Spend
```

**Inputs needed:**
- Total business revenue for the period
- Total marketing spend across all channels for the same period

**Output:** MER ratio with interpretation versus your business type benchmark.

| Business type | Healthy MER | Excellent MER |
|--------------|-------------|---------------|
| E-commerce | 3.0–5.0× | > 5.0× |
| SaaS | 5–10× | > 10× |
| Local service | 3–8× | > 8× |

<Tip>
  MER captures blended efficiency including organic, brand, and retention revenue — not just paid-attributed revenue. It's a more accurate measure of true marketing ROI than platform-reported ROAS, which overclaims by 20–40% due to attribution overlap between channels.
</Tip>

## Quick formula reference

| Metric | Formula |
|--------|---------|
| CPA | Spend ÷ Conversions |
| ROAS | Revenue ÷ Spend |
| CTR | Clicks ÷ Impressions × 100 |
| CVR | Conversions ÷ Clicks × 100 |
| CPC | Spend ÷ Clicks |
| CPM | (Spend ÷ Impressions) × 1,000 |
| CPL | Spend ÷ Leads |
| Break-even CPA | AOV × Margin% |
| Break-even ROAS | 1 ÷ Margin% |
| LTV | ARPU × Average Customer Lifespan |
| CAC | Total Marketing Spend ÷ New Customers |
| MER | Total Revenue ÷ Total Marketing Spend |
| Impression Share Opportunity | Revenue × (1 ÷ IS − 1) |
