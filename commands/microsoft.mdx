The `/ads microsoft` command runs a full Microsoft Ads audit covering Bing search, the Microsoft Audience Network, Copilot chat placements, and LinkedIn profile targeting. It evaluates 24 checks across five weighted categories and produces a `MICROSOFT-ADS-REPORT.md` with a health score, import validation findings, Copilot readiness assessment, and CPC comparison against Google. Microsoft Ads is often treated as a Google import and left unmonitored—the audit surfaces the specific risks and unique opportunities that make it worth dedicated review.

## Scope

`/ads microsoft` covers all major Microsoft Ads campaign and format types:

- Search campaigns on Bing, Yahoo, and syndicated partner networks
- Performance Max campaigns (up to 300 per account, with LinkedIn profile data integration)
- Multimedia Ads and Responsive Search Ads
- Copilot chat placements (73% higher CTR than traditional search)
- Connected TV inventory: Netflix, Max, Hulu, Roku, discovery+
- LinkedIn profile targeting across Search, DSA, Shopping, PMax, and Multimedia ads
- Microsoft-unique extensions: Action Extension, Filter Link Extension, Review Extension
- Vertical video (9:16, available since April 2025, up to 90 seconds)

    | Check | Description | Severity |
    |-------|-------------|----------|

    **Common import failures to verify:**

## Copilot integration

Microsoft's Copilot AI represents the largest differentiated opportunity in Microsoft Ads. Unlike traditional search, Copilot conversations involve users who are actively researching and problem-solving—making them significantly higher purchase intent than standard search clicks.

## Google import safety

Google Ads import is the most common Microsoft Ads setup method—and the most common source of account problems. The audit verifies the following import safety checklist:

- Conversion goals verified post-import (most common failure point)
- Tracking templates transferred and functioning
- Extensions fully imported (partial imports are common)
- Bid adjustments reviewed for Bing-appropriate levels (import targets are typically too high)
- **Scheduled auto-imports disabled** after initial setup to prevent silent campaign re-enablement
- Landing page URLs functioning on Microsoft Ads (different click parameters)

<Warning>
  Scheduled auto-imports are a Critical severity failure if running unmonitored. They can re-enable paused campaigns and overwrite manual bid/budget changes without any notification. Disable scheduled imports after the initial account setup.
</Warning>
