Running your first audit takes three things: Claude Code with Claude Ads installed, a data export from your ad platform, and a one-line command. Claude Ads handles the rest — asking for context, analyzing your data, and producing scored output files you can act on immediately.

## Run your first audit

<Steps>
  <Step title="Start Claude Code">
    Open your terminal and launch Claude Code:

    ```bash
    claude
    ```
  </Step>

  <Step title="Export your ad account data">
    Download a report from your ad platform dashboard covering the **last 30 days**. Claude Ads works with CSV exports, screenshots, or metrics pasted directly into the chat. You do not need API credentials.

    Recommended exports by platform:

    | Platform | What to export |
    |----------|---------------|
    | Google Ads | Campaign performance report (CSV) |
    | Meta Ads | Campaign, ad set, and ad-level breakdown (CSV) |
    | LinkedIn Ads | Campaign performance export |
    | TikTok Ads | Campaign report |
    | Microsoft Ads | Campaign performance report |
    | Apple Ads | Campaign dashboard screenshot or CSV |
  </Step>

  <Step title="Provide your context upfront">
    Before every audit, Claude Ads collects four pieces of context. You can answer the intake questions when prompted, or provide this information in your opening message to get better results immediately:

    1. **Industry / business type** — SaaS, ecommerce, local service, B2B enterprise, info products, mobile app, real estate, healthcare, finance, or agency
    2. **Monthly ad spend** — total and per-platform breakdown (approximate is fine)
    3. **Primary goal** — sales/revenue, leads/demos, app installs, calls, or brand
    4. **Active platforms** — which platforms you are advertising on

    <Tip>
      Providing context upfront produces better recommendations. A message like "I spend $5k/mo on Google Ads for a SaaS company, primary goal is demo requests" lets Claude Ads apply the right industry benchmarks and budget-appropriate checks without asking follow-up questions.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Run an audit command">
    Choose the command that matches your situation:

    <CodeGroup>

    ```shell Full multi-platform audit
    /ads audit
    ```

    ```shell Google Ads only
    /ads google
    ```

    ```shell Meta Ads only
    /ads meta
    ```

    ```shell Strategic plan for SaaS
    /ads plan saas
    ```

    </CodeGroup>

    Paste or attach your exported data when Claude Ads prompts for it.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Common first commands

| Command | What it does |
|---------|--------------|
| `/ads audit` | Full multi-platform audit — spawns 6 parallel agents for budget, compliance, creative, tracking, Google, and Meta simultaneously |
| `/ads google` | Google Ads deep analysis across Search, PMax, AI Max, Demand Gen, CTV, and YouTube (80 checks) |
| `/ads meta` | Meta Ads deep analysis for Facebook, Instagram, and Advantage+ Shopping (50 checks) |
| `/ads plan saas` | Strategic ad plan using the SaaS industry template — trial/demo focus, Google + LinkedIn platform mix, KPI targets |

For a full list of commands, see [Full audit reference](/commands/full-audit).

## What the output looks like

After analysis, Claude Ads produces three output files in your working directory:

| File | Contents |
|------|----------|
| `ADS-AUDIT-REPORT.md` | Per-platform scores, finding details, and severity breakdown |
| `ADS-ACTION-PLAN.md` | Prioritized list of fixes organized by critical, high, medium, and low severity |
| `ADS-QUICK-WINS.md` | Top changes estimated to have the fastest impact relative to effort |

For full audits (`/ads audit`), the report also includes an aggregate **Ads Health Score (0–100)** weighted by each platform's share of your total spend.

## Grading scale

| Grade | Score | What it means |
|-------|-------|---------------|
| A | 90–100 | Minor optimizations only — accounts in strong shape |
| B | 75–89 | Some improvement opportunities — good baseline, fixable gaps |
| C | 60–74 | Notable issues need attention — performance drag present |
| D | 40–59 | Significant problems — prioritize fixes before scaling |
| F | &lt;40 | Urgent intervention required — structural issues likely costing budget |

## Tips for better results

<Tip>
  Always include your industry and monthly budget when running any audit. Benchmarks and best practices differ significantly between a $500/month account and a $50,000/month account. Telling Claude Ads "I spend $2k/month on Google Ads for a local plumbing business" produces far more relevant recommendations than running `/ads google` without context.
</Tip>

<Note>
  Claude Ads auto-detects your business type from ad account signals (conversion events, platform mix, targeting patterns, product feeds), but providing it explicitly is faster and more reliable.
</Note>
